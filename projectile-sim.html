PHYSICS MODEL

The simulator solves the equations of motion numerically using Euler integration with a 10ms timestep, accounting for gravity, aerodynamic drag, and user-defined wind conditions.

Drag Force
F_d = ½ ρ C_d A v_rel²
Aerodynamic drag force proportional to the square of relative velocity. ρ is air density (1.225 kg/m³), C_d is drag coefficient, A is cross-sectional area.
Equations of Motion
aₓ = F_dx / m
aᵧ = F_dy / m − g
Net acceleration from drag components and gravitational acceleration (g = 9.81 m/s²). Forces resolved along each axis from the drag direction vector.
Euler Integration
vₙ₊₁ = vₙ + aₙ·Δt
xₙ₊₁ = xₙ + vₙ·Δt
Velocity and position updated each timestep Δt = 0.01 s. Simulation terminates when y ≤ 0 (ground impact).
Relative Velocity
v_rel = v − v_wind
Drag depends on velocity relative to the surrounding air mass. Wind vector subtracted component-wise before computing drag magnitude and direction.
Kinetic Energy
KE = ½ m v²
Energy carried by the projectile due to motion. Lost continuously to drag (heat). Displayed in real-time in the Energy tab alongside potential energy.
Initial Conditions
vₓ = v₀ cos(θ)
vᵧ = v₀ sin(θ)
Launch velocity decomposed into horizontal and vertical components from initial speed v₀ and angle θ measured from the horizontal.
